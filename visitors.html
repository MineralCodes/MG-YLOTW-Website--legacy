Visitors

Please feel comfortable in joining us however you wish!
We have many visitors, just come, no permission needed. If you are attending for religious studies or a cultural sensitivity class, we welcome you!

If you are new to a Hebraic type of service, there is a lot going on, we don’t want you to be overwhelmed!

Dancing:�  We dance in worship, and are happy when others join us, you don’t have to know anything, come as you are!
Children’s blessing: If you have children, we invite you to bring them to be blessed by our congregation during the children’s blessing.
Kippas or yarmulkes and talits: are a personal choice.
Tzitziot: are worn by some, male and female, and not worn by others, as each is lead by their own understanding. If you don’t know what these are, don’t worry about it.
Bibles: We don’t use any particular bible, though our teachers primarily refer to the original Hebrew and Greek manuscripts.
What do I wear? We don’t have a particular dress code. Wear whatever you are comfortable wearing to a worship service.
