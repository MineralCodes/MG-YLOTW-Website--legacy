CONSTITUTION OF YESHUA LIGHT OF THE WORLD

SABBATH FELLOWSHIP

Click here for a PDF

PREAMBLE WHEREAS, it is the express purpose of God to call out of the world a people for His own possession who shall constitute the Body or Congregation of Adonai Yeshua, built and established upon the foundation of the Emissaries and Prophets, Messiah Yeshua Himself being the Chief Cornerstone; and

WHEREAS, God having ordained that this Universal Body should find its expression in and through local congregations calling them to assemble together for worship, fellowship, counsel, and instruction in the Word of God, for the work of the ministry and for the exercise of those spiritual gifts and offices provided for in the Brit Chadasha (New Covenant), therefore

BE IT RESOLVED, that we whose names appear on the Congregational Roster, as of this date, do recognize ourselves as a local body, the following Constitution or Articles of Congregational Order this date these supplement the Articles of Incorporation and supersede any and all other rules of assembly and order not given in Scripture.

ARTICLE I. NAME

The name of this Body shall be Yeshua Light of the World Sabbath Fellowship, a Biblical Messianic Congregation in the city of Colorado Springs, the county of El Paso and the State of Colorado.

ARTICLE II. PURPOSE

Yeshua Light of the World exists for the primary purposes as given in the Tanakh and the Brit Chadashah of making disciples with the power and love of a First Century faith by:

Section1:UPREACH (Beth Tefillah House of Prayer)We reach up to God who first reached down to us by being:

a)YESHUA CENTERED in making Yeshua the Lord of our life, faith and ministry as the Messiah of Israel. We seek to make Him known in all we do believing that God revealed himself through His Son Yeshua, and that regeneration by the Spirit of God indwelling us through our trust in the person and work of Yeshua is absolutely essential as individuals and as community.

b)PRAYER DIRECTED through commitment to prayer corporately and as individuals for God’s leading, empowerment and blessing in all that we do (Ephesians 6:18).

c)WORSHIP EMPOWERED as the Body of Messiah in spirit and in truth (John 4:21-24 ), believing that worship is a testimony of our passion for Elohim (Deut. 6:5), is spiritual warfare (2 Chronicles 20:17-22) and provokes unbelievers to jealousy (Romans 11:14).

Section 2: INREACH (Beth Midrash House of Learning) We reach in among ourselves to build the body of Messiah through:

a) TEACHING disciples to demonstrate their love for Messiah and for Elohim by obeying His commandments as expressed in the TaNaKh and the Brit Chadeshah. We are committed to fulfilling the Messianic Mandate (Matthew 28:18-20) by expounding and strengthening our connection to our Biblical Hebraic roots which informs our faith and empowers us to communicate the truth with greater power and effectiveness.

b) SERVICE in a commitment through acts of love and service towards one another, looking to the first Messianic congregation as our model (Acts 2:42-47). We believe in the responsibility and blessing of giving tithes and offerings to God for the support of our congregation.

c) INTERDEPENDENCE by walking together in the biblical standards of holiness and righteousness, being accountable to God and to one another in the congregation and by being open to godly counsel from the wider Body of Messiah.

d) SPIRIT LED through Spirit-empowered ministry, recognizing that we are inadequate in ourselves to carry out ministry and can only exercise our spiritual gifting in the power of the Ruach haKodesh (Holy Spirit). We encourage one another to be filled and empowered daily by the Holy Spirit, operating in the gifts of the Spirit for mutual edification and the producing of lasting fruit in ministry.

Section 3: OUTREACH (Beth Besora: House of Good News) We reach out from ourselves to proclaim the truth of the Besora haGeulah, the Good News of Redemption through:

a) PROCLAMATION in both words and deeds that there is salvation in no other name than Yeshua the Messiah, the only Savior of Israel and the world.

b) RESTORATION by announcing our Hebraic heritage as found in the Tanakh and Brit Chadesah. Much of the Church has separated itself from its Hebraic roots resulting in a wrong understanding of Yeshua, weakness in the face of the world system, and a rejection of God’s instruction regarding life (Torah). We believe the Messianic congregation is at its core an expression of Jew and Gentile united into one new man (Ephesians 2:15).

ARTICLE III.  PREROGATIVES

Section 1: This congregation shall have the right to govern itself according to the standards of the Tanakh and the Brit Chadasha (the New Covenant), “endeavoring to keep the unity of the Spirit, in the bond of peace...till we all come in unity of the faith, and of the knowledge of the Son of God (Messiah), unto a perfect man, unto the measure of the stature of the fullness of Messiah,” (Ephesians 4:3,13) and in defense of the “faith once for all delivered to the saints.” (Jude 1:2).

Section 2: To have a membership which is determined by the local body and to discipline its members according to Scriptures. (Matthew 18; Romans 16:17; 1 Corinthians 5:11;2 Thessalonians 3:6,14; Titus 3:10 & etc.)

Section 3: To choose, or call its spiritual leader, elect its officers, and transact all other business pertaining to its life and conduct as a local body.

Section 4: To establish and maintain such departments and institutions as may be deemed necessary for the purposes as earlier enumerated.

Section 5: To purchase, or acquire by gift, bequest, or otherwise either directly, or as trustee, and to own, hold in trust, use, sell subject to securing of a court order, convey, mortgage, lease, as allowed by state statute, or otherwise dispose of any real estate or chattels, as may be necessary for the furtherance of its purposes, all in accordance with its constitution or as the same may be hereafter modified, or amended.

ARTICLE IV.  AFFILIATION

While maintaining its inherent rights to sovereignty in the conduct of its own affairs, this body may voluntarily enter into full cooperative fellowship with congregations and Messianic organizations of like belief.

ARTICLE V.  ARTICLES OF BELIEF

The articles of faith of Yeshua Light of the World are contained in a separate document noted in this constitution by reference.

ARTICLE VI.  OFFICERS

The officers of the congregation shall be: The Congregational Leader (the Pastor who is also the Chief Elder or Undershepherd) who is spiritual leader of the congregation and also shall serve as President of the Corporation by virtue of his office. Additional Officers include Elders and Shamashim who shall be appointed by the congregation upon recommendation for the purpose of serving the congregation. The number of officers shall be determined by congregational need.

ARTICLE VII.  MEMBERSHIP

Individuals shall be eligible for membership in this congregation who give evidence of a personal living faith in Adonai Mashiach Yeshua, who demonstrate a personal commitment to this congregation and its well-being, who voluntarily adhere to the Articles of Faith, and agree to be governed by its Constitution as herein set forth and according to the standards as set in the congregational Membership Agreement.

ARTICLE VIII.  MEETINGS

Section 1: Meetings for public worship shall be help primarily on Shabbat (Saturday) and may be held at other times in order to fulfill the Purpose of the congregation and as may be required to celebrate the Holy days and Feast days, in obedience to Scripture. Other public meetings shall be provided for under the direction of the Congregational Leader and the Elders.

Section 2: There shall be an annual business meeting of the body, at which times the reports of all officers shall be read and election of officers may take place. Annual business meetings will be held following announcement of the meeting two Shabbats prior to scheduled meeting date. All notes and reports shall be dated/filed into the corporate file for future reference.

Section 3: Special business meetings of the body may be called when necessary by theCongregational Leader, or by a majority of the Elders. Additionally, special business meetings may be called by presentation of need by not less than one-fourth of the membership of the body. The request is to be presented to the Elders and if called, an announcement of these meetings is to be made two Shabbats prior to the date of the meeting.

Section 4: Records. An accurate record/minutes shall be maintained of all Board and Congregational meetings. All records shall be such that they provide concise, adequate information, or documentation, that will enable proper records for congregational history and activities to be developed.

Section 5: The Elders shall meet monthly for the transaction of the routine business of the body. Quarterly, the Elders will report to the congregation on the progress, goals, and needs of the congregation.

Section 6: Quorum. A quorum is essential for administrative integrity in both business and board meetings and is necessary for a meeting to be held.

ARTICLE IX.  FINANCES

The congregation shall be financed according to the standards of scripture by tithes and offerings of its members and friends of the local body (Malachi 3:10; Matthew 23:23; Hebrews 7:4-9 & etc.).

ARTICLE X.  PROPERTY

Section 1: All property real, or chattel, shall be taken, held, sold, transferred, or conveyed in the
name of the body in accordance with Colorado State law.

Section 2: No real or chattel property of the body shall be sold, leased, mortgaged or otherwise alienated, without the same having been authorized by at least two-thirds vote of the membership present at a regular business meeting, or special business meeting of the body which has been duly called for the consideration of the proposal.

Section 3: The Congregational Leader and the Board of Trustees shall certify in such conveyance, lease, or mortgage, that the same has been duly authorized by the majority vote of the membership. Such certificates shall be held to be conclusive evidence thereof.

Section 4: In the event that the Congregation herein ceases to function as a body, then the said property, real or chattel, shall be distributed to similar organizations which support and are active in Messianic ministry and objectives.

ARTICLE XI.  AMENDMENTS

Amendments to this constitution may be made by two-thirds majority vote of the membership of the body, who are in attendance at any regular, or special meeting called for that purpose, provided that due notice of such proposed changes shall have been made at all services on at least two Shabbats (Saturdays) preceding the time for such a meeting.
